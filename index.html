About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate studying Electrical Engineering at Columbia University, under the supervision of Dr. Xiaofan Jiang. My current research focuses on the design of cyber-physical systems for energy efficiency in urban environments. I have done research both at Carnegie Mellon University with Dr. Natasa Miskov-Zivanov and Dr. Pulkit Grover, as well as at the University of Pennsylvania with Dr. Daniel D. Lee. Their guidance has led me to pursue my current interests in robotics, machine learning, and cyber-physical systems.

Undergraduate Research Assistant | Summer 2014

I worked with Dr. Daniel D. Lee and Christopher Clingerman at the University of Pennsylvania on dynamic search based planning. The original project included a mobile robot that could detect obstacles and push them; my task was to implement a planner that was flexible enough to account for changes in the environment. Initially, I began with implementing the fairly popular D* Lite planning algorithm, and later optimized it for memory efficiency, motion primitives, and non-holonomic constraints.

Embedded Systems Capstone Project

In my capstone class at CMU, my team designed and implemented a piano tutoring system. The system sits on a piano and displays laser dots on the piano keys which indicate which notes to be hit (based on pre-uploaded MIDI files). The system then detects through audio whether the player has hit the correct notes, and then displays the next set of notes if hit correctly.

Energy Footprinting

My first project as a PhD focused on a system for tracking and visualizing personal energy consumption, or energy footprint, in commercial buildings. We deployed energy monitoring sensors, a bluetooth localization system, and mobile applications in a two-floor testbed at Columbia University. The results from our work can be found in the BuildSys 2016 Paper and the BuildSys 2017 Paper.

ICSL, Python, Energy
